● 3 sets hold for 30-60 seconds
● Rest: 60 seconds between sets


Why Full Body Workouts for Beginners?

Eciency: Full body workouts allow beginners to target multiple muscle groups in a single session, making the most of their time at the gym.
Frequency: Since you're working each muscle group every session, you can train more frequently, promoting faster skill acquisition and muscle adaptation.
Balanced Development: Full body workouts help prevent muscle imbalances, as all muscle groups are targeted consistently.
Foundation Building: Beginners need to build a foundation of strength and endurance across their entire body.
Full body workouts are ideal for this purpose.


Importance of Proper Form:

Injury Prevention: Correct form minimizes the risk of injury. Learning the right techniques from the beginning sets a foundation for safe and eective training.
Muscle Engagement: Proper form ensures that you're targeting the intended muscle groups, maximizing the beneﬁts of each exercise.
Long-Term Success: Developing good form early on establishes habits that contribute to long-term success and progress in your ﬁtness journey.
Eciency: Performing exercises with the right form enhances the eciency of your workouts, leading to better results over time.

Full Body Beginner's Gym Workout Program:

Strength Training:

Exercise 1: Back Squats

● 4 sets of 8-10 reps
● Rest: 90 seconds between sets


Exercise 2: Bench Press

● 4 sets of 8-10 reps
● Rest: 90 seconds between sets


Exercise 3: Bent Over Rows

● 3 sets of 10-12 reps
● Rest: 60 seconds between sets


Exercise 4: Overhead Press

● 3 sets of 10-12 reps
● Rest: 60 seconds between sets


Exercise 5: Deadlift

● 4 sets of 6-8 reps
● Rest: 90 seconds between sets


Hypertrophy (Muscle Building) Accessories:

Exercise 1: Dumbbell Lunges

● 3 sets of 12 reps per leg
● Rest: 60 seconds between sets


Exercise 2: Dumbbell Flyes

● 3 sets of 10-12 reps
● Rest: 60 seconds between sets


Exercise 3: Pull-Ups

● 3 sets to failure
● Rest: 60 seconds between sets


Core Work

Exercise 1: Hanging Leg Raises

● 3 sets of 12-15 reps
● Rest: 60 seconds between sets


Exercise 2: Plank Variations

● 3 sets, hold for 45-60 seconds each
● Rest: 60 seconds between sets


Notes

● Ensure progressive overload by gradually increasing weights or intensity.


● Listen to your body, and adjust weights if needed to maintain good form


● Rest days are crucial for recovery; consider incorporating 1-2 rest days between workouts.


This program aims to balance strength, hypertrophy, and functional training, catering to the needs of intermediate gym-goers. Adjustments can be made based on personal preferences and ﬁtness goals. If unsure about any exercise or technique, seek guidance from a ﬁtness professional.
